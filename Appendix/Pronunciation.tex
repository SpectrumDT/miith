\indent 
This is the correct way to pronounce \Miith{}ian names and words. Pronunciation is given in IPA (International Phonetic Alphabet) as well as in my own makeshift phonetics (for those who don't know IPA). 









\section{Notes about IPA}
%Note: 
%In IPA I will use \txipa{[\rr]} to represent the sound of an English R (otherwise represented as \txipa{[\*r]}). 
As a non-standard convention, I will use \txipa{[(\rr)]} or \txipa{[(\gr)]} to indicate that the consonant may be silent (vanished, replaced by a long vowel or turned into a schwa, \txipa{[@]}), depending on dialect. 









\section{Notes about my makeshift phonetics}
[CH] is like in German \pex{ach}. 
[KH] is like in German \pex{ich}. 
[ZH] is like the \lett{SI} in English \pex{vision}. 
[DH] is as in English \pex{this}. 

[RH] is a guttural R like in German, French or Danish. 
[RRH] is similar to [RH] but longer, as in German \pex{zerreissen} (maybe). 
An [(R)] or [(RH)] in parantheses indicates that the consonant may be silent, depending on dialect. 

[A] or [AH] or [AA] is similar to the \lett A in \pex{father} and [\Aumlaut{}] is as in \pex{cat}. 
[EI] is as in \pex{say} and [AI] is as in \pex{sigh}. 
[E] or [EH] is as in \pex{net}. 
[Y] is a vowel, like the \lett Y in French \pex{lune} or the \lett{\Uumlaut} in German \pex{D\uumlaut{}sseldorf}. 
[OO] is as in \pex{fool}, [OW] is as in \pex{slow} and [AW] is as in \pex{dawn}.
